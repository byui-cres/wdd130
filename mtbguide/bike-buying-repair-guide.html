Bike Buying Tips

Choosing and buying your bike.

After all the saving and working hard to earn some extra cash to buy your dream mountain bike. You hurried up to the store but to your horror, there's so many bike models and types... What bike will you choose?

To get you started here are some tips:

  • Know the terrain you want to ride eg. road, trail, snow, etc. This can help you decide what bike you want to buy.
  • Now choose the bike you want to buy based on the terrain you want to ride your bike. Often time you want to ride that bike everywhere, in that case you may choose the hybrid or cross country bike and choose the appropraite tire for the terrained.
  • Pay attention to more specific details like the tire size, body size and handle size. How many speed you want etc. Picking the incorrect size will lead to some enjury while riding your bike
  • Road test a certain bike category of your choice and see if your comfortable with it, if not replace it
  • Select the model and color of your bike. Be sure that you've selected what you really want. This will be the ride you will ride everyday.
  • Before riding the bike you may need to relube, recheck and tupeup the bike just to make sure its road worthy.
  • After considering everything including the professional advice from a mechanic or bike expert finalized the purchase. Do not forget you still need to buy some gears like your helmet, clothing, pumps, tools, gadgets and all other accessories to complete your biking experience.

Bike Repair and Maintenance Tips

Rules of a thumb on bike maintenance

Properly maintain bike makes your riding a very good riding experience.

Consider the following guides to properly maintain your bike.

  • Always clean your bike. Dirt can damage your bike in the long run.
  • Check tightness of the bolts and gears you do not want them lose
  • Grease every moving parts, there's a saying "If it is threaded, grease it!"
  • Practice how to properly repair flat tires. This is a MUST!
  • Check tire pressure always before every ride.
  • Ride with the correct tire pressure.
  • When you disassemble something, you must keep track of the order of the parts you remove and put it back together as what it was. Use your phone by taking pictures while disassembling.
  • Use the proper tools for the right job. Using the incorrect tools may damage your precious bike.
